## Philosophy
<details>
<summary>All of my software shares some common themes...</summary>

### Plain Text and Readable

They mostly deal in plain-text.  Binary formats and databases hold work hostage.  Your hard work and your creations aren't replaceable.

Vendors know their software is replaceable, so they lock you in and replace it themselves anyway, without so much as an apology.  With simple human-readable formats that can be easily read or translated by hand or machine, your work can go anywhere.

### Command Line and OS-Agnostic

GUIs, APIs and the frameworks-of-the-month all come and go.  Operating systems and graphics card drivers change the requirements for drawing the same image and text and font on the same hardware every year.

Headless, command-line programs started by commands are so basic and inherent to computing that their power lies in their simplicity.  A headless program will always be indifferent to its environment, looking and behaving exactly the same way anywhere you use it.

### Portable and Single-Binary

No installers and, where possible, no external dependencies unless they're equally portable.  My apps are tiny and self-contained.

They can be stored or installed anywhere and carry their own documentation entirely inside themselves, all while in most cases remaining smaller than a decently sized JPEG.
</details>
